Section 3 — Teaching Practice

Teaching Practice

These are not just stories. They are evidence of who I am as an educator.

Story 01

The Girl Nobody Saw

A shy, quiet student who hid at the back of the classroom, barely spoke, and had no confidence. Her parents and teachers had given up on her. I stopped trying to "teach" her and started trying to understand her. Slowly, I built trust. By age six, she became one of the best students in the class. Now, at age seven, she attends a top school in Hong Kong.

Relationship before learning. You cannot reach a child you refuse to see.
Story 02

The Boy Everyone Gave Up On

A student with severe behavioural needs, removed from the classroom for three years. Labelled "cannot teach." I simply refused to give up on him. Six months of consistent expectations and patience. Now he can sit, listen, participate, and complete work independently. He wrote a story — and smiled proudly.

"Cannot teach" usually means "not yet reached." Every child has a door to dignity.
Section 4 — Technology & AI Literacy

Technology & AI Literacy

Honest. Fearless. Future-ready.

My Background

I hold an Irish SETU Level 8 Honours Bachelor's Degree in Networks, Crime and IT Security. My formal training is in complex systems. My passion is making them accessible to young learners.

How I Teach AI

Children are growing up in the digital age. I do not pretend AI does not exist, nor do I warn them that screens will hurt their eyes. I am honest: if you know how to use it, technology can help you. Screen time has limits, but purposeful use has value. Small doses beat blind prohibition.

Cybersecurity in the Classroom

My cybersecurity background is not just a certificate — it is a teaching tool. I bring real-world case studies into the classroom: WannaCry, social engineering, password hygiene. Students learn to protect themselves, not because they fear technology, but because they understand it.

I teach students to use technology well — not to protect them from it.
Section 5 — Bilingual Teaching Practice

Bilingual Teaching Practice

Technical subjects need EAL scaffolding. Every student deserves a pathway into computer science.

Teaching CS in a bilingual environment is more than translation — it is about building conceptual bridges. Every technical term is paired with support, so every learner can overcome language barriers. My method is about systematically removing those barriers.

My Method

Bilingual Keywords First — Every lesson begins with 3–5 key terms displayed in English and Chinese. Students see vocabulary before encountering concepts, preparing them for understanding.

Visual Scaffolding — Diagrams, flowcharts, and physical demonstrations reduce language dependence. Students can understand binary addition by moving physical cards, without reading a single sentence.

Sentence Starters — For extended written answers, I provide materials in both languages with sentence starters. This removes blank-page anxiety, letting students focus on demonstrating technical understanding.

Collaborative Learning

Pair Before Individual — Students discuss concepts in pairs before working independently (usually mixed language ability). This builds confidence and generates peer explanations, sometimes more effective than teacher explanations.

Bilingual Glossary Preview — Every curriculum programme includes a comprehensive glossary with English, Chinese characters, and Pinyin. Students reference these throughout the course.

Section 7 — Curriculum System

Four Integrated Curriculum Programmes

A seamless pathway from digital literacy through to university-level computer science.

🖥️

IGCSE Computer Science

CIE 0478

Theoretical foundations and programming fundamentals. A two-year curriculum covering Paper 1 (Theory) and Paper 2 (Problem Solving and Programming).

  • Binary, hardware, networks, security
  • Python programming and pseudocode
  • Five mock exams with gap analysis
📱

ICT Programme

CIE 0417 + Common Core

From digital literacy to advanced productivity. A nine-year spiral curriculum from Grade 2 through IGCSE ICT — word processing, spreadsheets, databases, and web design.

  • Age-appropriate digital citizenship
  • Microsoft Office / Google Workspace
  • HTML, CSS, and database skills
🎓

A-Level Computer Science

CIE 9618

Advanced theory, programming projects, and exam mastery. AS and A2 covering databases, Boolean logic, AI, and practical software development.

  • SQL, Boolean algebra, floating point
  • Programming project (NEA style)
  • University and career pathways
🌍

IB DP Computer Science

IB DP (2024+)

SL and HL pathways with internal assessment support. Topics 1–7, TOK connections, case study preparation, and a complete IA handbook with examples.

  • SL and HL differentiation
  • Complete IA guide with mark schemes
  • TOK and university pathway connections
