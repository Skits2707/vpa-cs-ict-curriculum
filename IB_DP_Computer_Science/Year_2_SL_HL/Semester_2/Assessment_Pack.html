2. Mock Examination Feedback Protocol

After each mock examination, students receive:

  1. Raw marks within 48 hours
  2. Topic breakdown showing performance by syllabus area
  3. Question-level analysis identifying specific weaknesses
  4. Targeted revision tasks for weakest areas
  5. One-to-one tutorial (10 minutes) to discuss action plan

4. Final Examination Predicted Grades

Predicted grades are based on:

  • Mock examination performance (40%)
  • Topic test trajectory (20%)
  • IA predicted mark (20%)
  • Teacher professional judgement (20%)

5. Special Consideration and Access Arrangements

EAL students with approved IB access arrangements may receive:

  • Extra time (usually 25%)
  • Bilingual glossary (school-provided, subject to IB approval)
  • Separate accommodation
  • Use of computer (if handwriting is a barrier)

Applications must be submitted to the IB Coordinator by the deadline.

6. Post-Examination Support

After final IB examinations:

  • Results day support (July)
  • Remark guidance if borderline
  • University admission support
  • Alumni network for CS students
