What is the HL Paper 3 Case Study?

English: The IB DP Computer Science HL Paper 3 is a 1-hour examination based on a pre-released case study issued by the IB approximately 6 months before the exam. The case study describes a real-world scenario involving computer science concepts, and students must answer questions that test their understanding, analysis, and application of those concepts. The case study is worth 20% of the HL grade and is unique to HL students — SL students do not sit Paper 3.

中文：IB DP计算机科学HL Paper 3是一场基于IB在考试前约6个月发布的预发案例研究的1小时考试。案例研究描述了一个涉及计算机科学概念的真实场景，学生必须回答测试其理解、分析和应用这些概念的问题。案例研究占HL成绩的20%，是HL学生独有的——SL学生不参加Paper 3。

How to Approach the Case Study

Phase 1: First Reading (Week 1)

Goal: Understand the scenario, identify key stakeholders, and map the main computer science concepts.
  • Read the entire case study without taking notes — get a holistic sense of the scenario
  • Identify: What is the organisation? What problem are they trying to solve? What technologies are mentioned?
  • Highlight or underline technical terms you don't fully understand
  • Create a concept map showing relationships between entities, technologies, and goals

Phase 2: Deep Research (Weeks 2–8)

Goal: Build deep knowledge of every concept mentioned in the case study.
  • Research each technical term using academic sources (IB CS textbooks, academic papers, reputable tech publications)
  • Understand not just what the technology is, but how it works, why it's used, and what its limitations are
  • Connect each concept to the IB CS syllabus topics (Topic 1–7)
  • Find real-world examples beyond the case study to deepen understanding

Phase 3: Synthesis & Application (Weeks 9–16)

Goal: Apply knowledge to hypothetical scenarios and practice exam-style questions.
  • Create practice questions based on the case study — "What if the organisation wanted to...?"
  • Work through past Paper 3 questions from previous case studies to understand question style
  • Form study groups to discuss and debate different aspects of the case study
  • Write model answers and compare with mark schemes

Phase 4: Examination Preparation (Weeks 17–20)

Goal: Refine exam technique, time management, and confidence.
  • Timed practice under exam conditions (1 hour, no notes)
  • Review all notes and condense to a single-page summary
  • Focus on areas of weakness identified in practice
  • Ensure you can explain every concept in the case study clearly and concisely

Research Strategies

Primary Sources

  • IB CS Subject Guide: Ensure you understand how the case study connects to syllabus topics
  • Official IB Case Study Document: Read every word carefully — questions are based on specific details
  • IB CS Textbooks: Kosta Tsiprous, David Watson, and other IB-approved texts often have case study chapters
  • Past Mark Schemes: Understand how marks are awarded for different types of responses

Secondary Sources

  • Academic Journals: IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, Google Scholar for peer-reviewed research
  • Reputable Tech Publications: Ars Technica, Wired, MIT Technology Review, Nature Computing
  • Organisation Websites: If the case study mentions specific companies or technologies, visit their official documentation
  • IB CS Teacher Resources: Share notes and resources with your teacher and classmates

Note-Taking Systems

The Cornell Method for Case Studies

Divide your page into three sections:

  • Cue Column (left, 25%): Key terms, questions, and syllabus references
  • Notes Column (right, 65%): Detailed notes, definitions, diagrams, and explanations
  • Summary (bottom, 10%): One-sentence summary of the page's content

Example for a case study about autonomous vehicles:

Cue ColumnNotes Column
Lidar
Topic 3
Sensor input		 Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging) uses laser pulses to create 3D maps of surroundings. In autonomous vehicles, it provides high-resolution spatial data. Limitations: expensive, affected by weather, limited range. Alternative: camera-based computer vision (Tesla approach). Syllabus link: Topic 3 — hardware, sensors, and control systems.

Digital Note-Taking Tools

  • Notion / Obsidian: Link notes together, create concept maps, embed diagrams
  • OneNote: Handwritten annotations on PDF case study, audio notes from discussions
  • Anki: Flashcards for key terms and definitions with spaced repetition
  • Miro / Mermaid: Visual diagrams showing system architecture and data flow

Practice Questions by Type

Type 1: Knowledge & Understanding (4–6 marks)

Example: "Explain how Lidar sensors contribute to the autonomous vehicle's navigation system described in the case study."

Approach:

  • Define the technology in your own words (1 mark)
  • Explain its specific function in the case study context (2 marks)
  • Describe how it interacts with other components (2 marks)
  • Mention a limitation or trade-off (1 mark)

Type 2: Analysis & Application (6–8 marks)

Example: "The case study mentions that the organisation is considering replacing Lidar with pure computer vision. Analyse the potential benefits and drawbacks of this decision."

Approach:

  • Identify 2–3 benefits with justification from the case study (3–4 marks)
  • Identify 2–3 drawbacks with justification (3–4 marks)
  • Use a balanced structure — don't just list, explain the impact

Type 3: Evaluation & Synthesis (8–10 marks)

Example: "Evaluate the ethical implications of using AI-driven diagnostic systems in the healthcare scenario described. Recommend and justify one policy to address your primary concern."

Approach:

  • Identify 3–4 ethical issues (privacy, bias, accountability, job displacement) (3–4 marks)
  • Analyse the severity and likelihood of each issue (2–3 marks)
  • Propose a specific, actionable policy (2 marks)
  • Justify why this policy addresses the primary concern (2–3 marks)

Time Management in the Exam

TimeActivityMarks Target
0:00–0:05 Read all questions carefully; identify which questions you can answer confidently —
0:05–0:20 Answer all short-answer questions (1–4 marks each) — these are typically knowledge-based and quick 12–15 marks
0:20–0:40 Answer medium questions (4–8 marks) — analysis and application questions 10–12 marks
0:40–0:55 Answer extended-response questions (8–10 marks) — evaluation and synthesis 8–10 marks
0:55–1:00 Review answers, check for completeness, add any missing details —
⚠️ Time Trap Warning: Do not spend too long on the first question. Many students lose marks because they run out of time for the higher-mark questions at the end. If a question is taking too long, mark it and move on — return if time permits.

Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

PitfallWhy It HappensHow to Avoid
Regurgitating the case study Students copy sentences from the case study without adding analysis Always explain why something matters, how it works, or what the implications are
Generic answers Students give textbook definitions without connecting to the case study Start every answer with "In the context of [organisation/scenario]..."
Ignoring limitations Students only describe benefits, ignoring drawbacks and trade-offs For every technology mentioned, ask: "What could go wrong? What are the costs?"
Missing ethical dimensions Students focus purely on technical aspects Always consider: privacy, security, bias, accessibility, environmental impact, social impact
Poor time management Students spend 20 minutes on a 4-mark question Allocate time proportionally: 1 mark ≈ 2 minutes. Move on if stuck.
No real-world examples Students only use the case study scenario Bring in external examples to show depth: "Similarly, Tesla uses..." or "In contrast, Amazon's approach..."
Weak evaluation Students list pros and cons without making a judgment End evaluation questions with a clear recommendation: "Therefore, the best approach is... because..."
Neglecting the syllabus Students research the topic but forget to link to IB CS concepts Constantly ask: "Which syllabus topic is this? How would the IB want me to explain this?"

Exam Technique Checklist

  • ☐ Read the case study at least 3 times before the exam
  • ☐ Know every technical term by heart
  • ☐ Practise writing answers under timed conditions
  • ☐ Use the case study organisation's name in your answers
  • ☐ Include diagrams where appropriate (system architecture, data flow, network topology)
  • ☐ Define terms before using them
  • ☐ Use structured paragraphs: Point → Evidence → Explanation → Link
  • ☐ For 6+ mark questions, plan your answer before writing
  • ☐ Leave 5 minutes at the end to review
  • ☐ Write legibly — examiners cannot mark what they cannot read
