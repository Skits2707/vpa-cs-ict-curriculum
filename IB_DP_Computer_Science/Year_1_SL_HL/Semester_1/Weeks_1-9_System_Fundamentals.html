Topic 1.1: Systems in Organizations 组织中的系统 Zǔzhī zhōng de Xìtǒng

Week 1 — Introduction to Systems 系统简介 Xìtǒng Jiǎnjiè

LessonContentActivitiesDifferentiation
1What is a system? Open vs closed systems. Systems in everyday life.Brainstorm: systems at VPA (timetable, canteen, library)Bronze: Label system diagrams; Silver: Describe a chosen system; Gold: Analyse system boundaries
2Components of an information system: hardware, software, data, procedures, people (硬件 yìngjiàn, 软件 ruǎnjiàn, 数据 shùjù)Match components to descriptions (bilingual cards)Bronze: Matching; Silver: Give examples; Gold: Evaluate missing component impact
3Roles in system development: Systems analyst, project manager, end-user, client (系统分析师 xìtǒng fēnxī shī)Role-play: interview a "client" about their needsAll: Participate in role-play with differentiated question scripts
4System life cycle models: Waterfall, Agile, RAD (瀑布模型 pùbù móxíng, 敏捷开发 mǐnjié kāifā)Compare models using a Venn diagramBronze: Fill Venn diagram; Silver: Explain one model; Gold: Evaluate which model suits a given scenario
5TOK: Can we ever fully understand a client's needs? Formative: Exit ticket on system componentsDiscussion + written reflectionAll: Contribute to discussion; Gold: Link to knowledge questions
TOK Knowledge Question: When a systems analyst gathers requirements, they construct a representation of reality. To what extent is this representation an accurate map of the client's world, and what ethical implications arise when the map diverges from the territory?

Topic 1.2: System Design Basics 系统设计基础 Xìtǒng Shèjì Jīchǔ

Week 4 — Modeling Systems: ER Diagrams 实体关系图 Shítǐ Guānxì Tú

LessonContentActivitiesDifferentiation
1Introduction to entity-relationship modeling: entities, attributes, relationships (实体 shítǐ, 属性 shǔxìng, 关系 guānxì)Identify entities in a school library systemBronze: Circle entities in description; Silver: List attributes; Gold: Define relationships with cardinality
2Cardinality and participation: 1:1, 1:N, M:N (基数 jīshù)Match relationship types to scenariosBronze: Match given; Silver: Determine from description; Gold: Resolve M:N into associative entities
3Constructing ER diagrams from written descriptionsGuided practice: VPA library system ER diagramScaffolded worksheets with decreasing support
4Constructing ER diagrams independentlyIndependent: Design ER for VPA cafeteria ordering systemBronze: 3 entities minimum; Silver: 4+ with correct cardinality; Gold: Normalisation to 3NF discussed
5TOK: Do abstractions like ER diagrams capture the truth of a system, or do they impose a particular way of seeing?Socratic seminarAll participate; Gold facilitates
TOK Knowledge Question: ER diagrams are simplifications of complex realities. When we reduce a real-world situation to entities and relationships, what knowledge do we gain, and what do we inevitably lose? Is a model that omits detail more or less true than the reality it represents?

Key Vocabulary (Topic 1)

English中文PinyinDefinition
System系统xìtǒngA set of interconnected components working together to achieve a purpose
Feasibility study可行性研究kěxíngxìng yánjiūAnalysis of technical, economic, and operational viability
Changeover系统切换xìtǒng qiēhuànThe process of replacing an old system with a new one
Entity实体shítǐA distinct object or concept about which data is stored
Relationship关系guānxìAn association between two or more entities
Cardinality基数jīshùThe number of instances in one entity related to instances in another
Flowchart流程图liúchéngtúA diagram representing the sequence of operations in a process
IPO table输入处理输出表shūrù chǔlǐ shūchū biǎoA table listing inputs, processing steps, and outputs for a system
Stakeholder利益相关者lìyì xiāngguān zhěAnyone with an interest in or affected by a system
Milestone里程碑lǐchéngbēiA significant point or event in a project timeline
