Semester Overview

Semester 1 of Year 1 covers Topic 1: System Fundamentals and Topic 2: Computer Organization. These foundational topics establish the conceptual framework for the entire IB DP Computer Science course and build directly on the IGCSE Computer Science (0478) knowledge students have acquired.

Learning Objectives (IB Command Terms)

By the end of this semester, students will be able to:

  • Define key terms related to systems and computer organization (定义 dìngyì)
  • Describe the stages of system development and the components of computer architecture (描述 miáoshù)
  • Explain how data is represented in binary and how the CPU executes instructions (解释 jiěshì)
  • Construct ER diagrams, flowcharts, and logic gate circuits (构建 gòujiàn)
  • Compare different memory types, storage devices, and logic operations (比较 bǐjiào)
  • Evaluate the suitability of different systems for given organizational contexts (评估 pínggū)

TOK Connections This Semester

Topic 1: To what extent can we know that a new system will solve the problems it was designed to address? How do models (ER diagrams, flowcharts) represent reality, and what do they omit?

Topic 2: If a computer processes everything as binary, how does meaning emerge from mere 1s and 0s? Is the map (architecture diagram) the territory (the physical machine)?

IA Preparation

During this semester, students will begin IA Stage 0: Contextual Awareness. They will:
  • Understand what constitutes a real client and a real problem
  • Begin documenting potential client contacts and problem areas
  • Review exemplar IAs from the IB
