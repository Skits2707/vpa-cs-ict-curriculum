VPA IB DP Computer Science — Year 1 Semester 2 Overview

Topics: 3 (Networks) + 4 (Computational Thinking) | Level: SL + HL

Semester Overview

Semester 2 builds on the foundations from Semester 1. Topic 3 (Networks) explores how computers communicate, while Topic 4 (Computational Thinking) develops programming and algorithmic skills essential for the IA and Paper 2. This semester includes intensive IA planning and early development.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain how data travels across networks using protocols and packet switching
  • Compare network topologies, structures, and security mechanisms
  • Construct algorithms using pseudocode and trace tables
  • Analyse the efficiency of sorting and searching algorithms
  • Apply software development methodology to the IA project

TOK Connections This Semester

Topic 3: The internet is often called a "network of networks"—but what does it mean to say information "travels"? Is data a thing that moves, or is this merely a useful metaphor? How do encryption and trust relate to knowledge claims in digital spaces?

Topic 4: An algorithm is a set of instructions that, followed mechanically, produces a result. In what sense does an algorithm "know" what it is doing? Can a correct algorithm be said to contain knowledge, or is knowledge only in the mind of the programmer?
