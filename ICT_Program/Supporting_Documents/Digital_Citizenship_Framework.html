🛡️ VPA Digital Citizenship Framework

Victoria Park Academy | ICT Programme
A comprehensive, age-appropriate framework for developing responsible digital citizens. Aligned with ISTE Standards for Students and Common Sense Education Digital Citizenship Curriculum.

Framework Pillars

1. Safety & Privacy 安全与隐私 — Protecting personal information and staying safe online
2. Respect & Kindness 尊重与友善 — Treating others with empathy and avoiding cyberbullying
3. Literacy & Critical Thinking 媒介素养与批判思维 — Evaluating information and recognising misinformation
4. Ethics & Responsibility 伦理与责任 — Understanding copyright, data ethics, and AI bias
5. Balance & Wellbeing 平衡与健康 — Managing screen time and maintaining digital wellness

Grade Band: G2–3 (Ages 7–8)

Focus: Safety, Kindness, Passwords

Learning Objectives

  • Identify personal information that should never be shared online (full name, address, school, phone number)
  • Create and remember a strong password using a memorable phrase
  • Describe what to do if something online makes them feel uncomfortable
  • Demonstrate kind and respectful communication in digital spaces
  • Recognise the difference between online friends and real-life friends

学习目标：识别不应在网上分享的个人信息；创建并记住强密码；描述在网上感到不适时的应对方法；在数字空间中展示友善和尊重的沟通方式；区分网友和现实中的朋友。

Key Activities

  • Password Superheroes: Students create a superhero identity and design a strong password based on their hero's catchphrase (e.g., "Spider-Man swings in 2024!" → SMsWi2024!)
  • Safe or Sorry: Sorting game where students classify information cards as "Safe to Share" or "Keep Private"
  • Kindness Chain: Students write positive digital messages on paper links; chain is displayed in the ICT lab
  • Trusted Adult Map: Students draw a map of trusted adults they can tell if something online worries them
  • Emoji Feelings: Using emoji faces to identify how different online scenarios might make them feel

主要活动：密码超级英雄——学生创建超级英雄身份并设计强密码；安全还是遗憾——分类游戏，将信息卡分为"可分享"和"保密"；友善链——学生在纸环上写下积极的数字信息；信任成人地图——画出可以倾诉的 trusted adults；表情感受——用表情符号识别不同网络场景的感受。

Assessment

  • Formative: Observation during sorting games; teacher checklist for password creation
  • Summative: Digital Safety Poster — students design a poster showing 3 rules for staying safe online (rubric: clarity, accuracy, visual appeal)
  • Peer Assessment: Students review each other's posters and add one positive suggestion

评估：形成性评估——观察分类游戏表现，检查密码创建；总结性评估——设计数字安全海报，展示3条网络安全规则；同伴评估——互相评价海报并给出建议。

Grade Band: G4–5 (Ages 9–10)

Focus: Privacy, Cyberbullying, Digital Footprint

Learning Objectives

  • Explain how websites collect data through cookies and forms
  • Define cyberbullying and describe strategies for bystander intervention
  • Understand that digital actions create a permanent footprint
  • Evaluate the appropriateness of content before sharing
  • Describe the difference between public and private online spaces

学习目标：解释网站如何通过Cookie和表单收集数据；定义网络霸凌并描述旁观者干预策略；理解数字行为会留下永久足迹；评估分享内容的适当性；区分公共和私人网络空间。

Key Activities

  • Cookie Trail: Students follow a simulated "cookie trail" showing how websites track preferences; discussion on privacy settings
  • Cyberbullying Scenario Cards: Role-play responses to cyberbullying situations using the STOP method (Stop, Tell, Offer support, Prevent)
  • Digital Footprint Art: Students create a footprint artwork where each toe represents a positive digital habit they will maintain
  • Privacy Policy Detective: Simplified analysis of a children's website privacy policy — what data is collected and why?
  • Think Before You Post: Students evaluate hypothetical social media posts using the THINK framework (True, Helpful, Inspiring, Necessary, Kind)

主要活动：Cookie追踪——模拟网站追踪偏好，讨论隐私设置；网络霸凌情景卡——用STOP方法角色扮演应对网络霸凌；数字足迹艺术——创作足迹艺术品；隐私政策侦探——简化分析儿童网站隐私政策；发帖前思考——用THINK框架评估假设的社交媒体帖子。

Assessment

  • Formative: Exit tickets after each activity; role-play observation rubric
  • Summative: Digital Citizenship Diary — 1-week reflection journal documenting online choices and their impact (rubric: reflection depth, honesty, action planning)
  • Peer Assessment: Partner review of diary entries with constructive feedback

评估：形成性评估——活动后出口票，角色扮演观察量表；总结性评估——数字公民日记，记录一周的网络选择及其影响；同伴评估——互相评阅日记并给予建设性反馈。

Grade Band: G6–8 (Ages 11–13)

Focus: Copyright, Misinformation, Critical Evaluation

Learning Objectives

  • Explain copyright, fair use, and Creative Commons licensing
  • Identify techniques used in misinformation and fake news
  • Evaluate the credibility of online sources using the CRAAP test
  • Understand how algorithms create filter bubbles and echo chambers
  • Describe the legal and ethical implications of plagiarism and piracy

学习目标：解释版权、合理使用和知识共享许可；识别错误信息和假新闻的常用技巧；用CRAAP测试评估网络来源的可信度；理解算法如何制造过滤气泡和回音室；描述抄袭和盗版的法律与伦理影响。

Key Activities

  • Copyright Court: Mock trial where students argue copyright cases (sampling music, using images in presentations, remixing videos)
  • Fake News Factory: Students create a deliberately misleading headline, then analyse how techniques (emotional language, false authority, cherry-picked data) manipulate readers
  • CRAAP Test Challenge: Teams evaluate 5 websites on Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose; present findings
  • Filter Bubble Simulation: Students search the same term on different accounts and compare results; discuss algorithmic bias
  • Creative Commons Scavenger Hunt: Find and correctly attribute images, music, and text using CC-licensed sources
  • Fact-Check Relay: Rapid-fire fact-checking of viral claims using Snopes, FactCheck.org, and primary sources

主要活动：版权法庭——模拟审判版权案件；假新闻工厂——学生制作误导性标题并分析操纵技巧；CRAAP测试挑战——团队评估网站可信度；过滤气泡模拟——不同账户搜索同一词汇比较结果；知识共享寻宝——寻找并正确标注CC许可资源；事实核查接力——快速核查 viral claims。

Assessment

  • Formative: Source evaluation worksheets; peer review of Creative Commons attributions
  • Summative: Misinformation Investigation Report — students investigate a viral claim, trace its origin, evaluate evidence, and write a 500-word report (rubric: research depth, source quality, argumentation, citation accuracy)
  • Peer Assessment: Cross-class review using structured feedback forms

评估：形成性评估——来源评估工作表，同伴评阅CC标注；总结性评估——错误信息调查报告，调查 viral claim 的起源和证据，撰写500字报告；同伴评估——跨班级结构化反馈。

Grade Band: G9–10 (Ages 14–15)

Focus: Data Ethics, AI Bias, Global Digital Citizenship

Learning Objectives

  • Analyse how big data collection impacts privacy and autonomy
  • Explain algorithmic bias and its societal consequences
  • Evaluate the ethical implications of AI in surveillance, employment, and healthcare
  • Understand data protection regulations (GDPR, PIPL) and their global impact
  • Propose ethical frameworks for technology development and use
  • Engage in informed debate about digital rights and responsibilities

学习目标：分析大数据收集如何影响隐私和自主性；解释算法偏见及其社会后果；评估AI在监控、就业和医疗中的伦理影响；理解数据保护法规（GDPR、PIPL）及其全球影响；提出技术开发和使用的伦理框架；参与关于数字权利和责任的知情辩论。

Key Activities

  • AI Bias Audit: Students test facial recognition or hiring algorithms with diverse datasets; document and present bias findings
  • Data Ethics Case Studies: Analyse real cases (Cambridge Analytica, facial recognition in China, algorithmic sentencing in US courts)
  • GDPR vs PIPL Debate: Structured debate comparing EU and Chinese data protection frameworks
  • Ethical Technology Design Sprint: Teams design an app with built-in ethical safeguards; present to a panel
  • Digital Rights Manifesto: Students draft a class manifesto on digital rights, vote on clauses, and publish on the school website
  • Guest Speaker Series: Invite data protection officers, AI researchers, and digital rights activists for Q&A sessions

主要活动：AI偏见审计——用多样数据集测试面部识别或招聘算法；数据伦理案例研究——分析真实案例；GDPR vs PIPL辩论——比较欧盟和中国数据保护框架；伦理技术设计冲刺——设计带有伦理保障的应用；数字权利宣言——起草班级数字权利宣言并发布；嘉宾演讲系列——邀请数据保护官员、AI研究员和数字权利活动家。

Assessment

  • Formative: Case study analysis sheets; debate participation rubric; design sprint peer feedback
  • Summative: Digital Ethics Position Paper — 1500-word argumentative essay on a chosen digital ethics topic (rubric: argument strength, evidence quality, counter-argument engagement, ethical framework application, academic citation)
  • Peer Assessment: Blind peer review using academic review criteria; revision based on feedback

评估：形成性评估——案例分析表，辩论参与量表，设计冲刺同伴反馈；总结性评估——数字伦理立场论文，1500字论证性文章；同伴评估——盲审学术评审标准，根据反馈修改。
