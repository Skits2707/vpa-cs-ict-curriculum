Week 10 — Mock Paper 1 (Theory) Part A

📋 Mock Paper 1 — Theory Examination

Duration: 2 hours | Conditions: Examination conditions (silent, no notes, no devices)

Format: This mock uses an authentic past paper or teacher-generated paper matching IGCSE 0417 Paper 1 style.

Section Breakdown:

  • Section A (30 marks): Short-answer questions (1–3 marks each) covering hardware, software, networks, security
  • Section B (30 marks): Medium-answer questions (4–6 marks each) covering data analysis, databases, programming concepts
  • Section C (40 marks): Extended-response questions (8–12 marks each) covering digital citizenship, systems analysis, emerging technologies

Pre-Exam Preparation:

  • Revision session: 1 hour on Monday reviewing key theory topics
  • Flashcard review: Students self-test using digital flashcards (Quizlet/Anki)
  • Mind map challenge: Create a mind map of the entire syllabus in 20 minutes

Week 12 — Mock Paper 2 (Document Production) Part A

🖥 Mock Paper 2 — Document Production Practical

Duration: 2 hours 30 minutes | Conditions: Practical examination conditions (individual work, no internet, no templates)

Format: Students receive a scenario and source files. They must produce:

  • Task 1 (30 marks): A fully formatted document (letter, report, or newsletter) with styles, tables, images, and mail merge
  • Task 2 (30 marks): A spreadsheet with formulas, functions, charts, and conditional formatting
  • Task 3 (20 marks): A presentation with appropriate design, transitions, and animations

Pre-Exam Preparation:

  • Speed drill: Complete a basic document formatting task in 15 minutes
  • Formula check: Rapid-fire formula writing (10 formulas in 10 minutes)
  • Presentation sprint: Create a 3-slide presentation in 10 minutes

Week 14 — Mock Paper 3 (Web & Database) Part A

🌐 Mock Paper 3 — Web Authoring & Databases Practical

Duration: 2 hours | Conditions: Practical examination conditions (individual work, no internet, no pre-built templates)

Format: Students receive a scenario and source files. They must produce:

  • Task 1 (40 marks): A multi-page website with HTML, CSS, navigation, forms, and embedded media
  • Task 2 (40 marks): A relational database with tables, relationships, queries, forms, and reports

Pre-Exam Preparation:

  • HTML/CSS speed test: Build a basic 3-page site in 30 minutes
  • SQL drill: Write 5 common queries in 10 minutes (SELECT, WHERE, ORDER BY, JOIN, aggregate)
  • Database design challenge: Design a 3-table database with relationships in 15 minutes

Week 15 — Mock Paper 3 Feedback & Targeted Revision

📊 Mock Paper 3 Feedback & Analysis

Lesson 1: Code Review Session (90 minutes)

  • Teacher displays exemplary HTML/CSS code and discusses best practices
  • Common HTML errors: missing closing tags, incorrect nesting, missing alt attributes
  • Common CSS errors: missing semicolons, incorrect selectors, unclosed braces
  • Common SQL errors: incorrect JOIN syntax, missing GROUP BY, wrong aggregate functions

Lesson 2: Debugging Workshop (90 minutes)

  • Students are given "broken" HTML pages and must fix them
  • Students are given "broken" SQL queries and must correct them
  • Use W3C validator to check HTML; test queries against expected output

Lesson 3: HTML/CSS & SQL Refinement (90 minutes)

  • Responsive design practice: Make a page mobile-friendly in 15 minutes
  • Complex query practice: Write queries with multiple JOINs and subqueries
  • Form validation: Add HTML5 validation attributes to a form

Week 17 — Final Examination Technique

⏱ Examination Technique Mastery
Paper 1 Technique:
  • Time allocation: 1 mark ≈ 1.2 minutes. A 3-mark question = 3–4 minutes. A 12-mark question = 14–15 minutes.
  • Command words: "State" = 1 sentence; "Describe" = 2–3 sentences with detail; "Explain" = cause and effect; "Evaluate" = pros, cons, and judgment.
  • Extended responses: Use PEEL structure (Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link) for 8+ mark questions.
  • Diagrams: Always draw diagrams when asked — a well-labelled diagram can earn 3–4 marks instantly.
  • Don't leave blanks: Even a partial answer can earn 1 mark. Write something relevant.
Paper 2 Technique:
  • File management: Save frequently (Ctrl+S every 5 minutes). Save with correct filenames as specified.
  • Task order: Start with your strongest task to build confidence and secure marks early.
  • Evidence document: Take screenshots of key steps (formulas, mail merge results, chart settings) as evidence.
  • Check, check, check: In the last 10 minutes, verify: all files saved, all tasks complete, no placeholder text remaining.
Paper 3 Technique:
  • HTML validation: Test your pages in a browser constantly. Check links work. Check images display.
  • CSS testing: Resize the browser window to test responsiveness. Check colours and fonts are consistent.
  • Database testing: Run every query and verify the output matches expectations. Test with edge cases (empty fields, duplicate data).
  • Backup: Keep a copy of your original source files in a separate folder before you start modifying.

Time Management Drills:

  • Monday: Paper 1 speed drill — 20 questions in 25 minutes
  • Tuesday: Paper 2 Task 1 speed drill — complete document in 50 minutes
  • Wednesday: Paper 2 Task 2 speed drill — complete spreadsheet in 50 minutes
  • Thursday: Paper 3 Task 1 speed drill — build 2-page site in 45 minutes
  • Friday: Paper 3 Task 2 speed drill — build database with 3 queries in 45 minutes

Week 18 — Final Preparation & Confidence Building

🌟 Final Week: Confidence & Readiness

Monday: Light Revision (60 minutes)

  • Review one-page summary notes for each paper
  • Quick-fire terminology test (50 terms in 20 minutes)
  • Watch "examination technique" video reminders

Tuesday: Peer Teaching (60 minutes)

  • Students teach each other their strongest topic in 5-minute micro-lessons
  • Gold students support Bronze students in targeted areas
  • Create a class "examination tips" poster

Wednesday: Mock Examination Reflection (60 minutes)

  • Students write a letter to their future self (start of Year 11) with advice
  • Class discussion: "What I learned from mocks that will help me succeed"
  • Celebration of progress: Compare diagnostic test (Week 1) with mock results

Thursday: Stress Management & Wellbeing (60 minutes)

  • Mindfulness and breathing exercises for examination anxiety
  • Physical preparation: sleep, nutrition, hydration advice
  • Examination day checklist: what to bring, what to do, what to avoid

Friday: Final Confidence Activity (60 minutes)

  • "Examination Ready" pledge: Each student signs a commitment to their preparation plan
  • Teacher distributes personalised "strengths cards" — one thing each student does excellently
  • Class photo and celebration of the semester's hard work
