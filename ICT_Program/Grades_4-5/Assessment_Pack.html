Grades 4–5 Assessment Pack

Program: Digital Fluency | Assessment Types: Formative (ongoing) & Summative (term-end)

Assessment Philosophy

Grades 4–5 assessment balances skill demonstration with project-based learning. Students build a digital portfolio throughout the year and complete timed practical assessments each term. EAL support includes bilingual rubrics, visual exemplars, and scaffolded tasks.

Digital Portfolio Requirements

By the end of Grade 5, each student's portfolio must contain:

  • ☐ Best document (formatted with table and images)
  • ☐ Best spreadsheet (with formulas and chart)
  • ☐ Best presentation (minimum 5 slides)
  • ☐ Scratch project (with documentation)
  • ☐ Research notes from one project
  • ☐ WPM growth chart (all 3 terms)
  • ☐ Self-reflection: "What ICT skill helped me most this year?"

Peer Assessment Forms

Document Review:

  • ☐ The document looks professional and organized
  • ☐ Text is formatted (bold/italic/color used well)
  • ☐ Pictures support the text
  • ☐ One thing I learned from this document: ________
  • ☐ One suggestion: ________

Scratch Project Review:

  • ☐ I could understand what to do without help
  • ☐ The sprites move and interact
  • ☐ There is a way to win or complete the task
  • ☐ My favorite part: ________
  • ☐ One idea to make it better: ________

EAL Support in Assessment

  • Bilingual rubrics: All assessment criteria available in English and Chinese
  • Visual exemplars: Bronze/Silver/Gold examples displayed before each task
  • Extended time: +10 minutes for Bronze-level students on practical tasks
  • Oral options: Students may explain their work orally instead of in writing
  • Sentence frames: Provided for all written reflections
